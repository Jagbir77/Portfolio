01 — Projects

Design & Engineering

A collection of work spanning CAD modelling, architectural design, and hands-on construction — from concept to reality.

CAD Construction Self-taught

Residential Staircase — From Drawing to Concrete

Designed a complete residential staircase by self-learning through YouTube tutorials. Created a detailed 2D CAD plan with precise dimensions — 10'8" total rise, 18'9" total run, 7" risers, and 10" treads at 33" clear width.

The design was then built by masons on-site as part of a larger house renovation project that I managed end-to-end.

CAD Assembly

Belt Tightener Assembly

A 10-part mechanical assembly featuring a frame, bracket, pulley, shaft, bushings, and fasteners — all modelled with precise tolerances and documented with professional engineering drawings.

Technical Drawing Detailing

Frame — Detail Drawing

Detailed engineering drawing of the belt tightener frame with section views (A-A, B-B), full dimensioning, and an isometric render at 1:1 scale.

FreeCAD Fusion 360

Additional CAD Work

Further projects include a bicycle stem, bicycle hub, HVAC duct system layout, and various mechanical components — created in FreeCAD and Fusion 360 using Part Design, Draft, Assembly, and Fasteners workbenches.

02 — Ventures

Creative Businesses & Experiments

Not every venture succeeds — but each one taught me something invaluable. Here are the businesses and experiments I pursued.

Augmented Reality Business 2024

Rubbal Studio — Augmented Reality Services

Launched an augmented reality business towards the end of college, offering custom social media filters and AR experiences for brands.

Built using JavaScript, Lens Studio, Spark AR Studio, and visual scripting. Created advertising videos with Photoshop, video editing tools, Canva, and incorporated 3D modelling for immersive effects.

Blender 3D Printing AI Tools

Photo-to-Relief Portrait Art

Developed a process to convert photographs into 3D pop-relief portrait artwork using Blender, AI tools, and 3D printing. Explored this as a business venture — a valuable learning experience in product-market fit.

Lesson: Great technique doesn't guarantee market demand. Validate before scaling.
Product Design Concept

Photo-to-Chocolate Concept

Experimented with turning photographs into chocolate moulds — combining food design with digital fabrication. Explored commercial viability but ultimately pivoted.

Lesson: Fail fast, learn faster. Every dead end sharpens your sense of direction.
03 — Journey

How I Got Here

A non-linear path through construction management, failed startups, and self-taught design.

House Renovation

Full Renovation Management

Took on full responsibility for a residential renovation project. Managed labour, controlled costs, coordinated masons and tradespeople, and made critical design decisions — including the staircase that I designed from scratch using YouTube tutorials.

This experience taught me to evaluate every decision through the lens of cost vs. investment — a mindset I carry into all my work.
